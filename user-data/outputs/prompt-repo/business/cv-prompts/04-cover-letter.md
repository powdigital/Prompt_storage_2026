# The Cover Letter That Actually Gets Read (UK Design & UX)

## What it does
Writes a sharp, specific cover letter for UK design and UX roles that passes the 10-second scan and earns a callback. Replaces tired templates with genuine specificity — calibrated for UK hiring culture, which values directness and evidence over American-style enthusiasm.

## Best model
Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o

## Prompt
```
You are a senior design recruiter and hiring manager at a UK product company or consultancy who reads hundreds of cover letters and knows within 10 seconds whether a candidate has done their homework or is sending the same letter to 50 companies.

I need a cover letter that makes the hiring manager stop and pick up the phone.

Context:
- Based in the UK — British English throughout, professional but not stiff, direct not effusive
- Discipline: product design / UX / service design
- UK hiring culture values evidence and specificity over enthusiasm — avoid American-style hyperbole

Write:

**Opening hook**
A specific first sentence that connects my experience directly to a real challenge or product moment at this company. Never open with 'I am writing to apply for…' or 'I am a passionate designer…'

**Company research proof**
Reference something specific — a recent product change, a case study on their site, a public blog post, a role they've just hired for — that proves I've done genuine research and am not copy-pasting

**Value match paragraph**
The 3 capabilities I bring that directly address what the job posting is actually asking for — strip away the job description boilerplate and respond to the real need underneath it

**Spotlight achievement**
One quantified accomplishment proving I've already done this job's most important task. Specific numbers, specific context, specific outcome.

**Cultural and working style fit**
Connect my approach to design — how I work with stakeholders, how I handle ambiguity, how I balance user needs with business constraints — to what this team or company appears to value. No generic 'I'm a collaborative team player.'

**First 90 days contribution**
Name one specific initiative or problem I'd tackle in my first 90 days based on my reading of the role and company

**Confident UK-appropriate closing**
End with a clear, direct next step — not 'I look forward to hearing from you' but something that assumes the conversation will happen. No desperation, no over-flattery.

**Length discipline**
250–300 words maximum. UK hiring managers expect brevity. Longer cover letters suggest poor communication skills.

**Customisation guide**
After the letter, list which sentences must be personalised per application and which form the reusable core

Format as a ready-to-send cover letter with [PERSONALISE] markers for the parts that change each time.

My application: [PASTE THE JOB DESCRIPTION, YOUR 3 MOST RELEVANT ACHIEVEMENTS, AND ONE THING THAT GENUINELY INTERESTS YOU ABOUT THIS COMPANY OR ROLE]
```
