# The Salary Negotiation War Room (UK Design & UX)

## What it does
Builds a complete salary or day rate negotiation strategy for UK design and UX roles — covering market benchmarking, counter-offer scripts, total compensation breakdown, and how to handle the specific awkwardness of UK salary conversations, which are more reserved than US equivalents but equally negotiable.

Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o

## Prompt
```
You are a senior compensation consultant and career coach who specialises in UK design and technology roles — helping product designers, UX designers, and design leaders negotiate salaries and day rates that reflect their actual market value, not just what the first offer said.

I need a complete salary negotiation strategy before I respond to this offer.

Context:
- Based in the UK — all figures in GBP, British English throughout
- Discipline: product design / UX / service design
- May be negotiating a permanent salary or a contract day rate

Research and benchmark:

**UK market rate context**
The realistic salary or day rate range for this specific role, seniority, sector, and location in the current UK market. Reference relevant benchmarks (e.g. Design Week salary survey, Aquent/Vitamin T, LinkedIn Salary Insights, Glassdoor UK) and note London vs. regional variance.

**Total compensation breakdown (permanent roles)**
Base salary + annual bonus + equity or LTIP + pension contribution + private healthcare + professional development budget + remote/hybrid flexibility + holiday entitlement. Never negotiate on base alone.

**Day rate benchmarking (contract roles)**
IR35 status implications, inside vs. outside IR35 rate differential, umbrella vs. limited company considerations, and how to factor holiday, sick pay, and pension into the day rate calculation.

**Leverage inventory**
Which of my achievements, skills, or competing opportunities justify pushing toward the top of the range — portfolio of shipped products, specialist sector knowledge, certifications, design system expertise, leadership experience.

**Counter-offer script**
The exact words to use when they make the first offer — acknowledge, don't react, ask for time if needed, then come back with a specific number and brief rationale. Rule: never accept the first offer without at least one counter.

**The silence approach**
After stating a counter, stop talking. The discomfort works in your favour. This is culturally relevant in UK negotiations — it doesn't have to be aggressive to be effective.

**Beyond-salary negotiation**
If base or day rate is fixed: negotiate remote days, additional annual leave, signing bonus, earlier salary review, professional development budget, title upgrade, or flexible working pattern.

**Lowball response**
Word-for-word script when the offer comes in significantly below your range — how to respond professionally without closing the door or accepting a bad deal.

**Email counter template**
A professional follow-up email that documents your counter with brief supporting rationale — useful when you want to negotiate asynchronously or create a paper trail.

**Walk-away threshold**
Framework for calculating the minimum total compensation you'll accept — and when declining and continuing to search is the smarter decision.

Format as a negotiation playbook with market context, counter-offer scripts, an email template, and a decision framework.

My offer: [DESCRIBE THE ROLE, COMPANY, OFFERED SALARY OR DAY RATE, YOUR CURRENT OR MOST RECENT COMPENSATION, YOUR YEARS OF EXPERIENCE, AND ANY COMPETING OFFERS OR CONVERSATIONS]
```
