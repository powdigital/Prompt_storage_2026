# The ATS Robot-Proof CV Optimiser (UK Design & UX)

## What it does
Reverse-engineers how applicant tracking systems used by UK employers score and rank CVs. Optimises keyword placement, formatting, and structure so your CV survives automated screening and reaches a human reviewer — calibrated for product design, UX, and service design roles.

## Best model
Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o

## Prompt
```
You are a senior applicant tracking system consultant who has reverse-engineered how Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, Taleo, and SmartRecruiters score and rank CVs — because the majority of CVs are rejected by software before a human ever sees them.

I need my CV optimised to score in the top 10% of ATS systems at UK employers.

Context:
- Based in the UK — use British English throughout (CV not resume, programme not program, organise not organize)
- Discipline: product design / UX / service design / design leadership
- Target employers include UK tech companies, consultancies, financial services, and public sector digital teams (HMRC, DVLA, NHS Digital, GDS)

Optimise for:

**Keyword extraction**
Pull every critical term from the job description that the ATS will scan for — including both design-specific terms (user research, interaction design, service blueprinting, design systems, accessibility) and role-generic terms (stakeholder management, agile, roadmap)

**Exact match placement**
Embed each keyword naturally into experience bullets, summary, and skills section — avoid keyword stuffing, which modern ATS systems penalise

**Synonym and acronym coverage**
Include both full versions and abbreviations: 'User Experience (UX)', 'Interaction Design (IxD)', 'Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)', 'Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)'

**Section heading compliance**
Use only standard headings ATS systems recognise: Profile, Experience, Education, Skills, Certifications — avoid creative headings like 'Where I've Made an Impact'

**Formatting purge**
Remove all tables, columns, text boxes, graphics, headers, footers, icons, and special characters that crash ATS parsers. Design CVs are particularly prone to this — even beautifully designed PDFs fail ATS entirely

**File format guidance**
Submit .docx for ATS portals (most compatible). Use .pdf only when applying directly to a human or via email. Never submit a designed PDF through an ATS portal

**Date format consistency**
Use 'Month Year – Month Year' format throughout so parsers correctly calculate tenure and identify gaps

**Job title alignment**
Match title wording to the target role without fabricating experience — 'Senior Product Designer' vs 'Lead UX Designer' can affect match scoring significantly

**Design skills taxonomy**
Prioritise hard skills ATS scans for: Figma, Sketch, Prototyping, User Research, Usability Testing, Design Systems, Accessibility, Service Design — above soft skills it typically ignores

**ATS match score estimate**
Rate my optimised CV from 1–100 against the specific job description with a breakdown of matched and missing terms

Format as an ATS-optimised CV with a keyword match report showing every term added and where it was placed.

My target: [PASTE YOUR CURRENT CV AND THE EXACT JOB DESCRIPTION YOU'RE APPLYING FOR]
```
