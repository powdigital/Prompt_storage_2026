# The LinkedIn Profile Transformer (UK Design & UX)

## What it does
Rewrites your entire LinkedIn profile to attract inbound opportunities from UK design recruiters and in-house hiring managers. Optimises for how recruiters actually search — by skills, titles, and keywords — while building a coherent professional narrative for anyone who lands on your profile.

## Best model
Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o

## Prompt
```
You are a senior design recruiter at a UK executive search and talent firm who fills Head of Design, Principal Designer, and Lead UX roles — and you know that most designers have LinkedIn profiles that describe what they do, not why someone should hire them.

I need my entire LinkedIn profile rewritten to attract the right recruiters and inbound opportunities in the UK design market.

Context:
- Based in the UK — British English throughout
- Discipline: product design / UX / service design / design leadership
- Target roles: senior IC, lead, principal, or head-of level positions at UK tech companies, fintechs, consultancies, or public sector digital teams

Transform:

**Headline formula**
[Seniority + Discipline] | [Specialism or sector] | [Value proposition or signature outcome]
Example: 'Senior Product Designer | Fintech & Enterprise SaaS | Turning complex systems into products people actually want to use'
Not just a job title — a reason to click

**About section**
Three paragraphs:
1. Who I work with and the problems I specialise in solving
2. How I work — methods, approach, what I bring to a team
3. What I've delivered — 2–3 concrete outcomes with numbers, and what I'm looking for next
End with a clear call to action (open to opportunities, happy to connect, etc.)

**Experience section**
Mirror CV achievements but with slightly more narrative — LinkedIn allows context that a CV doesn't. Each role: one sentence of context, two or three outcome-led bullet points.

**Featured section**
Curate 3–5 items: case studies, portfolio links, talks, articles, press coverage, or side projects. These are the first thing a recruiter clicks after your headline.

**Skills section**
Select skills that match how UK design recruiters search: Figma, User Research, Interaction Design, Service Design, Design Systems, Prototyping, Usability Testing, Accessibility, Stakeholder Management, Agile. Prioritise endorsable hard skills over soft ones.

**Keyword strategy**
Embed the exact job titles and discipline terms UK recruiters type into LinkedIn Recruiter: 'UX Designer', 'Product Designer', 'Service Designer', 'Design Lead', 'Head of UX' — whichever apply to your target roles

**Recommendation strategy**
Who to ask (managers, senior stakeholders, cross-functional peers), how to ask (a specific template message), and what to brief them to focus on so the recommendation is useful not generic

**Open to Work settings**
Whether to use the public banner or private recruiter-only signal, and which job titles and locations to specify

**Content and visibility strategy**
What to post or comment on weekly to stay visible in recruiter feeds — calibrated for someone who doesn't want to become a LinkedIn content creator but wants to stay active

Format as a complete LinkedIn optimisation guide with every section rewritten, a keyword list, and a simple 30-day visibility plan.

My LinkedIn: [PASTE YOUR CURRENT HEADLINE, ABOUT SECTION, AND MOST RECENT EXPERIENCE ENTRY]
```
