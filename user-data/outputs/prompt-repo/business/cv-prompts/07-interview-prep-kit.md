# The Design Interview Domination Prep Kit (UK)

## What it does
Builds a complete interview preparation system for senior UK design roles — covering company research, competency deconstruction, portfolio presentation strategy, STAR stories, and the specific questions UK design hiring panels ask. Treats interview prep as a structured design problem, not a guessing game.

## Best model
Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o

## Prompt
```
You are a senior interview coach who prepares candidates for design leadership and senior IC interviews at UK tech companies, consultancies, financial services firms, and public sector digital teams — where one weak portfolio walkthrough or vague answer to a stakeholder question costs the offer.

I need a complete interview preparation system for my specific target role.

Context:
- Based in the UK — British English throughout
- Discipline: product design / UX / service design
- UK interview culture: evidence-based, less performative than US equivalents — panels want specificity, not enthusiasm

Prepare:

**Company deep dive**
10 critical things I must know before walking in: product strategy, recent releases or pivots, leadership team and design org structure, known design challenges, competitors, culture signals (Glassdoor, team blog, job ads), and how design is positioned relative to product and engineering

**Role deconstruction**
Break the job description into 5 core competencies the panel is actually testing for — strip away the generic requirements and identify what this specific team genuinely needs

**Portfolio walkthrough strategy**
How to structure a case study presentation for a UK design panel: problem framing → research approach → key decisions and trade-offs → outcome and evidence → what I'd do differently. Emphasis on decision-making rationale, not just visual output.

**STAR story bank**
10 pre-scripted stories covering: leading through ambiguity, influencing without authority, navigating stakeholder conflict, advocating for users against business pressure, delivering under constraints, failure and recovery, mentoring or growing a designer, cross-functional collaboration, and shipping something you're proud of

**'Walk me through your process' answer**
A structured, honest 3-minute answer that reflects how I actually work — not a textbook Double Diamond recitation

**'Tell me about yourself' script**
A 90-second answer connecting past experience, current expertise, and why this specific role is the right next step — not a CV readthrough

**'Why this company' answer**
Specific, researched reasoning that demonstrates I understand what the design team is actually working on — not surface-level flattery about their brand

**Handling the 'greatest weakness' question**
A real, credible answer that shows self-awareness and active growth — not the classic 'I work too hard'

**Salary and rate handling (UK context)**
How to respond to 'what are your salary expectations?' in the UK market — whether to give a range, deflect to market rate, or ask what budget they have. Covers both permanent and contract/day rate contexts.

**Questions to ask them**
5 insightful questions demonstrating strategic thinking — about design maturity, team structure, how decisions get made, what success looks like, and what's blocking the team right now

**Red flag avoidance**
The mistakes that quietly kill UK design interviews: being too process-heavy without showing outcomes, talking about aesthetics without evidence, failing to demonstrate business awareness, not asking questions, or being unable to discuss a project that went wrong

**24-hour follow-up email**
A brief, specific thank-you note that references something from the conversation and reinforces one key point about your candidacy

Format as a complete interview preparation guide with scripted answers, STAR story templates, portfolio walkthrough structure, and a pre-interview checklist.

My interview: [DESCRIBE THE COMPANY, ROLE, INTERVIEW FORMAT (PORTFOLIO REVIEW / COMPETENCY / DESIGN CHALLENGE), AND YOUR BIGGEST CONCERN ABOUT YOUR CANDIDACY]
```
