# The Job Search System (UK Design & UX)

## What it does
Builds a complete, systematic job search operation for senior UK design roles — replacing the demoralising scroll-and-apply approach with a structured process that generates interviews predictably. Covers UK-specific job boards, recruiter relationships, warm outreach, and the weekly rhythm that keeps momentum without burning out.

## Best model
Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o

## Prompt
```
You are a senior job search strategist specialising in the UK design and product industry — you understand that finding a senior design role is a design problem: it needs research, prototyping, iteration, and a system, not random effort.

I need a complete job search system that generates interviews consistently.

Context:
- Based in the UK — British English throughout, UK-specific platforms and recruiters
- Discipline: product design / UX / service design
- Targeting senior IC, lead, principal, or head-of roles

Systematise:

**Target company list**
How to identify 30–50 specific UK companies worth targeting based on design maturity, culture fit, sector, and growth stage. Sources: Design Week, BIMA, Tech Nation, LinkedIn company pages, Glassdoor design team reviews.

**UK job board and platform strategy**
Where to focus attention in the UK market:
- LinkedIn Jobs (primary for senior roles)
- Workable and Greenhouse (via company career pages directly)
- Contra, Toptal, YunoJuno (for contract and freelance)
- We Work Remotely, Remote OK (for remote-first roles)
- Specific sector boards: FinTech jobs, NHS Jobs, GDS LinkedIn, public sector design networks
- Community job boards: UX Design UK Slack, Designer Hangout, Mind the Product

**Application tracking**
A simple spreadsheet system: company, role, date applied, source, status, next action, follow-up date, notes. Keeps you from losing track across 30+ live applications.

**Daily job search routine**
A 60-minute daily structure that covers applications, outreach, and pipeline maintenance — without consuming the whole day or creating anxiety through constant scrolling.

**Warm outreach to hiring managers**
How to identify and contact the design director, head of product, or CPO at target companies directly — before a role is advertised. LinkedIn message template included. This is where most senior roles are actually filled.

**UK design recruiter relationships**
Which specialist design recruiters are worth building relationships with in the UK market — how to approach them, what to send, and how to stay on their radar without being annoying. Key firms include Aquent, Vitamin T, Major Players, Zebra People, Hatch, and onefinestay talent for senior creative roles.

**Referral generation**
How to activate your existing network for introductions — a specific message template that makes it easy for contacts to refer you without feeling like you're asking for a favour.

**Application quality vs. volume decision**
When to write a fully tailored application (target companies, dream roles, warm leads) vs. when to apply at volume with a strong standard CV (safety net applications on high-volume boards).

**Portfolio and case study readiness**
Ensure your portfolio, LinkedIn, and a leave-behind PDF are consistent and current before ramping outreach — senior roles often progress fast once contact is made.

**Weekly review rhythm**
A 30-minute Friday review to assess conversion rates at each stage (applications → responses → interviews → offers), identify what's working, and adjust the strategy — treat it like a product sprint retrospective.

Format as a job search operations manual with daily workflow, tracking spreadsheet structure, outreach templates, and a recruiter contact list framework.

My search: [DESCRIBE YOUR TARGET ROLE AND SENIORITY, PREFERRED SECTORS OR COMPANY TYPES, LOCATION PREFERENCE (LONDON / REMOTE / HYBRID), AND HOW MANY HOURS PER WEEK YOU CAN DEDICATE TO THE SEARCH]
```
