# The Company Values CV Aligner (UK Design & UX)

## What it does
Rewrites your CV to demonstrate cultural and values alignment with a specific target employer — going beyond keywords to show that how you work matches what this company actually cares about. Especially useful for mission-driven organisations, companies with strong design cultures, and public sector roles where values fit is assessed explicitly.

## Best model
Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o

## Prompt
```
You are a senior design recruiter who evaluates CVs not just for skills and experience, but for evidence that a candidate's working style, values, and approach align with how this specific organisation operates — because a technically strong designer who clashes with the culture is a hire that fails within a year.

I need my CV aligned with the values and culture of my target employer.

Context:
- Based in the UK — British English throughout
- Discipline: product design / UX / service design
- UK employers range from high-growth fintechs to established consultancies to public sector digital teams, each with meaningfully different cultures

Align:

**Company values research**
Identify the 5–8 core values or working principles this employer publicly promotes — from their careers page, design blog, job ads, Glassdoor reviews, and any published design principles

**Bullet point mapping**
Tag each CV achievement with the company value or principle it demonstrates — make the alignment visible and deliberate

**Coverage gap scan**
Identify which company values have no representation in my current CV and feel genuinely absent from my experience

**Gap-filling bullets**
Write 3–5 new achievement statements drawn from my real experience that address the gaps — framed through the employer's language

**Sector-specific alignment notes:**

*UK public sector / GDS / NHS Digital*
Emphasise: user-centred design rigour, accessibility compliance (WCAG 2.1 AA), evidence-based decision making, open working, cross-government collaboration, and meeting the GOV.UK Service Standard

*UK fintech / financial services*
Emphasise: design within regulatory constraints, trust and transparency in UI, balancing speed-to-market with compliance, data-driven iteration, and experience with complex financial products

*UK consultancy / agency*
Emphasise: client management, multidisciplinary facilitation, design thinking workshop delivery, adapting to varied client cultures, and commercial awareness

*UK tech scale-up*
Emphasise: design at pace, wearing multiple hats, shipping in resource-constrained environments, founding or growing a design function, and contributing to product strategy

**Values-first professional summary**
Rewrite the profile/summary section to immediately signal cultural alignment in the first two sentences — without sounding like you've just read their About page

Format as a values-aligned CV with annotations showing which company principle each bullet demonstrates, plus a rewritten summary.

My target: [PASTE YOUR CV, THE TARGET COMPANY NAME, AND THEIR PUBLISHED VALUES, DESIGN PRINCIPLES, OR JOB DESCRIPTION]
```
