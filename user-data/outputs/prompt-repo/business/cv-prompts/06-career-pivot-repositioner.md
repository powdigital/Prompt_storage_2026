# The Career Pivot Repositioner (UK Design & UX)

## What it does
Completely repositions your CV for a pivot into or within the design field — whether you're moving from a related discipline (marketing, engineering, research), transitioning between design specialisms (agency to in-house, UX to service design), or targeting a different sector (finance, health, public sector). Translates transferable skills into the language of your target role.

## Best model
Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o

## Prompt
```
You are a senior career transition coach specialising in the UK design industry, with deep experience helping people move into or across product design, UX, service design, and design leadership roles.

I need my CV and narrative completely repositioned for a new role or sector.

Context:
- Based in the UK — British English throughout
- Discipline pivot: either entering design from an adjacent field, or moving between design specialisms or sectors
- UK design market — target employers may include fintechs, consultancies, NHS/public sector digital teams, scale-ups, or enterprise software companies

Reposition:

**Transferable skill extraction**
Identify every skill from my current experience that directly applies to my target design role — including research, synthesis, facilitation, systems thinking, stakeholder communication, and visual or analytical skills that cross disciplines

**Language translation**
Rewrite industry-specific jargon into the vocabulary my target design discipline uses. For example: 'client requirements gathering' becomes 'stakeholder discovery and requirements definition'; 'A/B testing marketing copy' becomes 'hypothesis-driven experimentation with quantitative validation'

**Achievement reframing**
Present the same accomplishments through the lens of what design teams value: user outcomes, cross-functional collaboration, problem framing, iterative delivery

**Gap identification**
Name the 2–3 specific skills or experience types I'm missing for this role, with realistic and UK-accessible ways to address them (e.g. Google UX Certificate, IxDF membership, NHS Design System contribution, GOV.UK service assessment volunteering, personal portfolio projects)

**Portfolio bridging**
If I don't have traditional design portfolio pieces, suggest how to frame existing work (documents, presentations, research outputs, process artefacts) as design evidence

**Professional summary pivot**
Rewrite my profile/summary to position me as a natural fit for the target role — leading with the most relevant transferable strengths, not apologising for what I lack

**Experience reorganisation**
Restructure the CV so the most transferable achievements appear first within each role, and minimise or contextualise less relevant history

**Career narrative**
Write a concise 30-second verbal summary explaining the pivot in a way that sounds intentional and strategic — for use in interviews, networking conversations, and the LinkedIn About section

**LinkedIn alignment**
Suggest headline and About section updates that reinforce the new direction consistently across CV and profile

Format as a pivot-ready CV with before (current framing) and after (target role framing) comparison for each section, plus the career narrative script.

My pivot: [DESCRIBE YOUR CURRENT ROLE AND BACKGROUND, TARGET ROLE AND SECTOR, AND WHY YOU'RE MAKING THIS CHANGE]
```
