# The Achievement Quantifier (UK Design & UX)

## What it does
Transforms vague design CV bullets into hard-evidenced achievement statements with metrics, scale, and business impact. Built for designers who struggle to quantify work that feels qualitative — research, ideation, facilitation — into language that hiring managers and procurement panels respond to.

## Best model
Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o

## Prompt
```
You are a senior CV strategist who specialises in helping UK product designers, UX designers, and service designers prove measurable business impact — because design work is often dismissed as subjective until it's expressed in the language of outcomes.

I need every bullet point on my CV transformed into a quantified achievement with hard numbers or credible scale indicators.

Context:
- Based in the UK — use British English throughout
- Discipline: product design / UX / service design
- If exact metrics aren't known, use honest scale indicators (e.g. 'enterprise platform serving 40,000 users', 'reduced average task time from 4 minutes to 90 seconds')

Quantify using these design-relevant lenses:

**User and business outcomes**
Did my work improve conversion, retention, task completion, NPS, CSAT, or error rates? Express as percentage change or absolute numbers.

**Scale and reach**
How many users, customers, or citizens did the product or service affect? Include DAU/MAU, total registered users, or transaction volume where known.

**Time and efficiency savings**
Did I speed up a user journey, internal process, or delivery cycle? Express as time saved per interaction, per week, or as percentage reduction.

**Revenue and cost impact**
Did my work influence revenue growth, cost reduction, support ticket deflection, or churn reduction? Translate design decisions into financial language.

**Team and delivery leadership**
How many designers did I lead, mentor, or hire? What was the delivery cadence — sprint velocity, features shipped per quarter, design system components delivered?

**Research rigour**
How many participants in research studies? How many rounds of testing? What was the confidence level of findings used to drive decisions?

**Stakeholder and organisational scope**
How many stakeholders, business units, or product teams did I work across? Was this a single product or a platform serving multiple lines of business?

**Accessibility and compliance**
Did I take a product to WCAG 2.1 AA compliance? How many accessibility issues were identified and resolved?

**The transformation formula**
Apply to every line: 'Delivered [WHAT] for [WHO], resulting in [MEASURABLE OUTCOME] by [DESIGN METHOD OR APPROACH]'

Format as rewritten achievement statements with the original bullet and the quantified version side by side for every line.

My bullets: [PASTE ALL YOUR CURRENT CV BULLET POINTS EXACTLY AS WRITTEN]
```
